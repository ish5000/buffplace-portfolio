Overview
Motivation

College life is scattered across too many platforms — none of them made for students. I wanted to help design something that brings it all into one place, with built-in trust that comes from knowing everyone on the platform is a verified CU student.

What I Did
Led paper prototyping research · Designed marketplace sub-flow · Built individual wireframes · Created persona (Luddite Larry)
The Concept
A verified student-only marketplace for buying, selling, roommate finding, and housing — all in one app, accessed through Buff Portal.
How Might We

How might we create a verified, student-only platform that combines a marketplace for buying, selling, and trading goods with a centralized hub for discovering campus events and organizations — so students can find what they need, connect with peers, and stop juggling five different apps.

User Research

What We Found

Four methods, four team members. Each approach covered a different layer of the problem.

01
Interviews — Cadence
Two 30-min interviews with CU students. Clear theme: generic platforms attract anyone in Boulder, not just students. Users wanted CU email verification and faster in-app messaging.
02
Observations — Sophia
2 hours across Facebook Marketplace, Depop, Poshmark, and Craigslist. Each platform serves one niche — no single option covers the full student need.
03
Surveys — Conor
20+ respondents. Confirmed strong market interest and validated that a student-specific identifier (Buff Portal login) would meaningfully improve trust.
04
Paper Prototyping — Ismael
Tested early UI concepts through four iterations. Key finding: forcing users to choose "Buy" or "Sell" on entry was an unnecessary barrier. Removing it in favor of open browse + categories felt dramatically more natural.
Key Takeaways
01
Strong reselling demand. Proven, active market for peer-to-peer commerce — especially at semester's end when students are moving out and giving things away.
02
Social discovery already works. About half of students find organizations easily. The opportunity is consolidation — centralizing what already exists, not reinventing it.
03
Trust needs a student identifier. A Buff Portal login or SID link solves both the verification and friction problem at once — turning a security concern into a feature.

Pain Points

What Testing Revealed

01
How does purchasing get logged?
Testers weren't sure how a completed deal would appear under "Purchased Items." The path from chat → offer → completion needed a clearer confirmation state.
02
What happens with a low seller rating?
Users wanted to know if a bad rating affected listing visibility or trust. The rating system needed a visible feedback loop — what a score actually means on the platform.
